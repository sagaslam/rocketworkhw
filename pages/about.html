OUR SUBSYSTEMS

At Rocket Works, we believe rocketry is a team effort, every part of the rocket is connected, and one design choice can affect the whole project. That’s why our subsystem teams aren’t isolated groups working on their own; instead, we encourage open communication and collaboration across all areas. While each member specialises in a certain subsystem, we also promote learning from one another, so everyone gains a broader understanding of the rocket to allow the team to become more adaptable and allows integration between subsystems.

ENGINEERING

Airframe

The Airframe team brings the rocket to life by designing and building its main structure and aerodynamic features. From shaping the rocket’s body to ensuring it can withstand powerful thrust and flight loads, this team turns ideas into the physical model. If you enjoy hands-on engineering, structural design, and seeing your work take shape in the real world, Airframe is where rockets become reality.

Flight Dynamics

Flight Dynamics is all about predicting, simulating, and controlling how our rocket flies. Using powerful software and real flight data, this team makes sure the rocket is stable, safe, and hits its target apogee. If you like physics, coding, problem-solving, and seeing numbers turn into real-world results, Flight Dynamics puts you in charge of the rocket’s journey.

Propulsion

The Propulsion team delivers the power behind our rockets. From integrating commercial solid motors for our first EuRoC launch, to developing our very own liquid rocket engine, this team is where engineering meets fire and thrust. If you’re fascinated by engines, fuel, and pushing the limits of what’s possible, Propulsion is the team for you.

Avionics

Avionics is the brain and nervous system of the rocket. This team designs and builds the electronics that control, monitor, and communicate with the rocket during flight. From telemetry and tracking to recovery and stability systems, Avionics makes sure we know exactly what’s happening at every stage. If you’re into coding, electronics, or solving complex challenges, this is the perfect place to innovate.

Payload

The Payload team focuses on what the rocket carries, from experimental satellites like CubeSats to creative mission concepts. This is the team where imagination meets engineering, developing payloads that generate valuable data and showcase innovation. If you want to design experiments that fly to the edge of space, Payload offers a chance to push science and technology skyward.

OPERATIONS

Outreach

Outreach is the team’s voice to the outside world. Whether it’s securing sponsorships, building partnerships, or engaging with the university and industry, this team helps ensure Rocket Works has the support and recognition it needs. If you’re confident, organised, and enjoy making connections, Outreach is where you can make a real impact beyond engineering.

Media

The Media team is all about telling our story. From managing our social platforms to creating videos, photos, and updates, they keep the world engaged with our progress. If you’re creative, enjoy content creation, or want to build a professional media presence, Media is your chance to shape how Rocket Works is seen.

Logistics

The Logistics team keeps everything moving smoothly, from organising test days and travel to ensuring the rocket and equipment get where they need to be, safely and on time. They also develop checklists and procedures that keep launches professional and secure. If you’re highly organised, enjoy planning, and want to be at the centre of making launches happen, Logistics is key to mission success.
