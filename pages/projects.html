ROCKET WORKS

OUR VISION

Rocket Works is developing two projects. Project Skye is our first student-built rocket, designed with CAD, FEA, and CFD tools, and powered by a commercial solid motor to compete at the European Rocketry Challenge (EuRoC) in Portugal in 2026.

Running in parallel, Project Lomond is our long-term ambition: the design, build, and hot-fire of a student-developed bi-propellant liquid rocket motor, with the goal of competing in Race2Space and entering the SRAD-Liquid category at EuRoC 2027. Together, these projects lay the foundation for Rocket Works to push the boundaries of student rocketry in the UK.

Project Skye

Skye is our first major rocket build, where the team will design, engineer, and launch a high-powered solid-motor rocket capable of reaching altitudes of up to 9 km. The project combines advanced CAD design, FEA and CFD validation, and hands-on manufacturing to deliver a competition-ready rocket. The desired outcome is a successful launch at the European Rocketry Challenge (EuRoC) in October 2026.

Project Lomond

Lomond is our long-term endeavour to design, build, and hot-fire a bi-propellant liquid rocket motor. Alongside technical research into fuel and oxidiser combinations, the project aims to develop, test, and validate a functioning liquid engine capable of powering a rocket for competition. The desired outcome is to compete at Race2Space in the summer of 2027 and enter the SRAD-Liquid category at EuRoC the following October.
